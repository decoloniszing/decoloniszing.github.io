decoloniszing our bookshelves


The purpose of this database is simple- to build an accessible catalogue of literature that receives data from local bookstores and libraries from all around the world. We want to design a website that puts accessibility before aesthetic-- by which we mean the purpose of the website is to make literature by people of marginalised and intersectional identities easily available and provide independent, left-leaning bookshops with the visibility they lack.

Search Advanced Search

What challenges are we facing?


  1. Lack of awareness and access to books by authors of marginalised and intersectional identities.
  2. Capitalist, hegemonic way of distributing books which focus on cis, white male narratives or focus on catering to a white/Western audience.
  3. Algorithms that are ingrained with racist, sexist and colonial biases hence recommend and promote only a certain kind of literature
  4. Lack of disability-friendly / accessible resources
  5. Uncatalogued works of literature, and literature that has been suppressed or censored.

What sort of literature are we cataloguing?


We are looking for books that are by (in no particular order and continuously updated):

  1. Adivasi Authors
  2. Authors who write in languages other than English
  3. Authors from the LGBTQIA+ community
  4. Bahujan authors
  5. Black authors
  6. Dalit authors
  7. Indigenous authors
  8. Persecuted religious minorities
  9. Refugees
  10. Stateless authors/ authors from a warzone/ a military occupied area
  11. Tribal Authors
  12. Women

We are looking for literature in every field and genre- right from data science to hybrid poetry.

While we want the catalogue to be an exhaustive repository of literature by diverse authors, we realise that people can be problematic. We plan to tackle this by having a disclaimer which emphasizes on the fact that an author may have expressed problematic views.


We do acknowledge that the lines can get blurry in the process but because this project is community-driven, we will always prioritise the emotional and mental well-being of our community over the inclusion of problematic discourses.

Please note that some authors will be on low priority, while some will definitely not be on the list. We will be actively collecting feedback to maintain transparency and accountability.

Low priority:
  1. Are they white, cis, male or all of the above, e.g., Jordan Peterson?
  2. Are they a globally mainstream author, e.g., Charles Bukowski? (defined by global readership, reportage by mainstream Western media)
  3. Have they explicitly directed hate towards a particular community, e.g., J K Rowling?


Exclusion from the list:
  1. Have they been directly involved in genocidal acts/ mass murder, e.g., Aung San Suu Kyi?
  2. Have they inspired/ incited genocide/ mass murder, e.g., Adolf Hitler?
  3. Have they been accused/ proven guilty of sexual crimes, e.g., Pablo Neruda?

In the event, an author has been excluded from the list, and an entity or individual makes a case for them to be on the list, we will be open for a discussion.
